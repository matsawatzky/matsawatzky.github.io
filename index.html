My Journey

I am a computer science student at The University of Winnipeg and an exercise scientist dedicated to exploring and building solutions to problems using a data driven, scientific approach. I am a lifelong student driven by the desire to continue learning in order to find the answers to more and more interesting questions.


B.A. Applied Computer Science

University of Winnipeg 2025
Thanks to an amazing set of proffesors I have fallen in love with the field of computer science and the amazing possibilities within it.

The University of Winnipeg has also provided many exciting oppurtunites including the chance to meet some amazing, and like minded people. Including participate in, and winning, The 2023 University of Winnipeg holiday hackathon with an amazing team.

Exercise Physiology and Biomechanics Research Assistant

University of Winnipeg 2020-2023
Working in a lab based setting on projects with national and international groups was a dream come true. During my time in the lab I contributed to several research studies, including presenting prelimary findings at the Canadian Society of Exercise Physiologists national conference, as well as co-authored an open source textbook on applied statistics for the Universities brand new Kinesiology Honours program. It also exposed me to the fascinating field of computer science for which I will forever be greatful as it led to where I am today.

B.Sc. Exercise Science

University of Winnipeg Earned 2021
It was during this degree that I fell in love with data and how it can be used to inform decisions. Taking advanced course in applied biomechanics, exercise physiology and nutrition ensured that I developed a rigorous and exacting approach to problem solving.

I was lucky enough to be awarded a higghly sought after Nation Sceiences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) grant to pursue a summer research term in the field of Exercise Physiology during my final year of school. It gave me so many amazing oppurtunites academically and to meet a lot of scientists at the tops of their fields.
